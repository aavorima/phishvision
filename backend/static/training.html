Red Flags to Watch For

Sender Address

Check the full email address, not just the display name

Urgency Pressure

Demands for immediate action are a major red flag

Suspicious URLs

Hover over links to verify the destination before clicking

Poor Grammar

Spelling mistakes and odd phrasing suggest fraud

Protect Yourself

1

Verify the sender

Always check the actual email address behind the display name. Attackers often use names that look legitimate.

2

Hover before you click

Move your cursor over any link to preview where it actually leads. Look for misspellings or unusual domains.

3

Go direct when uncertain

If an email claims to be from a service you use, open a new browser tab and go directly to that website.

4

Report suspicious emails

Forward potential phishing attempts to your IT security team. Your report could protect your colleagues.
